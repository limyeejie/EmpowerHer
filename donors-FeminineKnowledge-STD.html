What is STD?

Sexually transmitted diseases (STDs) are caused by sexually transmitted infections (STIs). They are spread mainly by sexual contact. STIs are caused by bacteria, viruses or parasites. A sexually transmitted infection may pass from person to person in blood, semen, or vaginal and other bodily fluids.

STD Symptoms

STD symptoms can vary depending on the type of infection, but common symptoms may include genital sores, discharge, pain during urination, and itching. It's important to be aware of these symptoms and seek medical attention if you experience any of them.

How to Prevent STD

Preventing STDs involves practicing safe sex, using condoms, and getting regular screenings.

STD Stages

STDs may progress through different stages, and early detection is key for effective treatment. Learning about the stages of specific STDs can help individuals understand the potential risks and consequences associated with each infection.

When to See a Doctor

If you suspect you may have been exposed to an STD or are experiencing symptoms, it is important to see a healthcare professional promptly. Early diagnosis and treatment can prevent complications and the spread of infections.
